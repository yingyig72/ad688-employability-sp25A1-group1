7. Career Strategy

7.1 Answer to the Research Question

AI career growth is strongest in large, diversified metros with defense, finance, and corporate services sectors — Washington DC, New York, and Dallas–Fort Worth lead in our data, followed by Chicago, Atlanta, and San Francisco. At the state level, California, Texas, Virginia, Washington, Massachusetts, New York, and DC consistently rank highest for AI hiring. Broader non-AI job growth is strongest in Sun Belt labor markets — especially Florida and Texas — with Dallas–Fort Worth and Austin among the most prominent metros. Texas stands out as the only state performing strongly on both dimensions.

This answer is based on our analysis of 72,454 Lightcast job postings (May–Sep 2024) and validated against UMD-LinkUp AI Maps, BLS employment data, and Lightcast’s 2025 Talent Attraction Scorecard.

7.2 Geographic Targeting Strategy

Based on the geographic and salary patterns we found, here are five practical strategies depending on what you prioritize:

Strategy Target Locations Trade-off
Maximum AI Volume Washington DC, NYC, Dallas–Fort Worth Large diversified markets — AI embedded in defense, finance, and corporate services, but high cost of living in DC and NYC
AI + Tech Ecosystem San Francisco, Seattle, Boston, San Jose Traditional tech hubs with deep AI ecosystems — highest salaries but also highest cost of living
AI + Affordable Living Austin, TX; Atlanta, GA; Raleigh, NC Growing AI ecosystems with moderate costs — not the highest volume, but strong upward trends
Bridge (AI + Broad Growth) Dallas–Fort Worth, TX; Austin, TX Both strong AI volume AND broad labor market growth — the most versatile option
Non-AI Stability + Growth Tampa, FL; Miami, FL; Phoenix, AZ; Jacksonville, FL Fast-growing labor markets driven by population and services — lower AI intensity but many non-AI career options

7.3 Skills Investment Plan

Our skill gap analysis (Section 5.3) identified the areas where market demand most exceeds our team’s current proficiency. Based on those gaps, here are the priority skills to develop, ranked by gap severity:

Priority Skill Why It Matters How to Build It
1 Machine Learning Appears in ~35% of AI postings in our data; our team averages 2.4/5 Online courses (Andrew Ng’s ML Specialization on Coursera), hands-on Kaggle projects
2 Communication Appears in ~43–50% of both AI and non-AI postings; our team averages 4.0/5 but market demand is very high Presentation practice, technical writing, cross-functional project experience
3 Deep Learning / NLP Growing demand in AI postings; our team averages 1.4/5 Fast.ai course, HuggingFace tutorials, build a small NLP project
4 Cloud Computing / AWS Increasingly required for deploying AI models; our team averages 2.0/5 AWS Cloud Practitioner (beginner, ~$100, 2–4 weeks), Azure Fundamentals AZ-900 (free, 1–2 weeks)
5 Docker / Spark Low team proficiency (1.2/5) and growing market presence Docker getting-started tutorial, Databricks community edition for Spark

These priorities come directly from the gap between what our Lightcast data shows employers are asking for and where our team self-assessed as weakest (Figures 12 and 13).
